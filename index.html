The three sounds of -ed

In regular past tense, the spelling is usually -ed, but the sound changes. Learn the quick test, then drill it until your ear catches it automatically.

t no voice

Use t after quiet endings like p, k, f, s, sh, and ch. Your throat does not vibrate.

walked = walkt washed = washt helped = helpt
d voice

Use d after vowels and voiced endings like b, g, v, z, m, n, l, and r. Your throat vibrates.

opened = open·d played = playd cleaned = cleand
id extra beat

Use id only after a verb that ends in a t or d sound. This is the only one that adds a syllable.

wanted = want·id needed = need·id started = start·id

Quick test: first ask, “Does the verb end in t or d?” If yes, say id. If no, touch your throat on the final sound. If it vibrates, say d. If it does not, say t.
